# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary reader is a senior decision-maker — VP Design, Chief Design Officer, or design executive — evaluating whether Nikhil can operate at Head / Director / VP-level product design leadership. They arrive skeptical, time-poor, and allergic to portfolio theater; they are judging organizational judgment, not screens.

Secondary readers, in priority order: executive recruiters (need fast, scannable credibility), founders and product/engineering leaders (need proof of business impact and building ability), and design leaders / AI-native teams (need reusable frameworks and operating guides). The site must be clear enough for a recruiter, deep enough for a VP, and useful enough for a practitioner — without flattening itself to please all of them.

Canonical source: `FOUNDATION/05_AUDIENCE.md`.

## Product Purpose

An executive knowledge system — a digital headquarters, transformation archive, and design-leadership operating manual — documenting how AI-native product organizations are built. It is a proof system for senior leadership roles and a long-term career asset, not a portfolio.

Positioning it must serve: **Nikhil Sharma is an AI-native Product Executive who builds organizations, systems, and businesses.** Success looks like the primary reader believing: "Nikhil can build the operating system of a product design organization."

Canonical sources: `FOUNDATION/03_POSITIONING.md`, `README.md`, `MASTER_PLAYBOOK.md`.

## Brand Personality

Central belief: **design creates systems that create products** — great products are the visible output of invisible systems, and the site reveals those systems.

Three words: composed, evidentiary, editorial.

Emotional goals: executive clarity, calm confidence, strategic depth. The tone pairs are the contract — sharp not loud, premium not luxurious, editorial not decorative, technical not cold, human not casual, confident not self-promotional, timeless not trendy. The active visual voice is dark kamran (see `DESIGN.md`): a single-column, dark, typographic operating manual with serious digital authority, not startup gloss or magazine cosplay.

Canonical source: `FOUNDATION/04_BRAND_THESIS.md`.

## Anti-references

- A Behance portfolio or Dribbble gallery — screenshot grids and visual polish without decision context.
- A resume website — chronology is not identity.
- A generic UX case study collection — no problem/process/solution/impact template unless elevated into a transformation narrative.
- A trendy AI landing page or startup SaaS template — AI is not the point; organizational transformation is.
- An overdesigned personal-brand vanity site — usefulness over self-celebration.
- Forbidden language: "passionate about," "world-class," "visionary," "cutting-edge," "pixel-perfect," floating buzzwords, unexplained screenshots, metrics that cannot survive interview scrutiny.

Canonical source: `ANTI_GOALS.md`.

## Design Principles

1. **Every section answers a hiring question.** Each page or section must teach, prove, clarify, or demonstrate judgment/capability/business impact. If it doesn't answer an executive hiring question, it doesn't get built.
2. **Evidence over assertion.** Metrics are proof, not decoration — gated by the claims register and evidence-ledger rendering rules. Never invented, never exaggerated, always ready for interview scrutiny.
3. **Typography carries the brand.** Authority comes from type, spacing, rhythm, and hierarchy — never from decorative visuals. Think essay, operating manual, archive, and field notes; not gallery.
4. **White space is a leadership signal.** Calm, composed pacing over density. Senior design does not crowd or shout.
5. **Timeless over trendy.** Every visual choice must still feel credible five years out. When in doubt, remove decoration and increase clarity — the strongest version feels inevitable, not impressive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA intent, treated as part of craft: semantic HTML with proper heading order, full keyboard navigation with visible `:focus-visible` states (2px ink), sufficient contrast, alt text for meaningful images, no text embedded in images, no horizontal overflow, responsive mobile-through-desktop (mobile is its own art-direction pass, not compressed desktop), and reduced-motion support for every animation.
