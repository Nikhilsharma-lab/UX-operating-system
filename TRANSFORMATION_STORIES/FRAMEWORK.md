# FRAMEWORK.md

## Purpose

Defines the canonical 16-part structure of a transformation story. Transformation stories replace case studies. They communicate transformation, decisions, and tradeoffs — not process.

A transformation story is not a UX case study. It is a senior leadership proof artifact. It must show business context, organizational complexity, constraints, research, decisions, tradeoffs, execution, measurable outcomes, lessons, reusable frameworks, and connections to the larger knowledge system.

## Why Transformation Stories, Not Case Studies

A case study shows a process: problem, process, solution, impact.

A transformation story shows judgment: a system or business moved from one state to another, the decisions that moved it, and what was learned.

Executives are hired for judgment. The structure below forces judgment to the surface.

## Canonical Transformation Story Structure

Every transformation story must follow the 16-part structure defined in `MASTER_PLAYBOOK.md`.

### 1. Context

What was happening in the business, product, organization, or market?

### 2. Business Problem

What business outcome needed to change?

### 3. Organizational Challenge

What team, stakeholder, process, alignment, maturity, or operating-model challenge made this difficult?

### 4. Constraints

What constraints shaped the work?

Include constraints such as:

- Regulation
- Legacy systems
- Engineering limitations
- Compliance
- Time
- Stakeholder alignment
- Customer literacy
- Operational realities
- Data limitations

### 5. Research

What evidence informed the work?

Include:

- Field research
- Customer interviews
- Behavioral observation
- Support data
- Funnel data
- Stakeholder inputs
- Product analytics
- Compliance/legal inputs where relevant

### 6. Decisions

What important decisions were made?

This is the most important section.

Directors are hired for judgment.

Show the decision logic, not just the output.

### 7. Tradeoffs

What was sacrificed, delayed, simplified, or rejected?

Show that leadership requires choosing.

### 8. Execution

How was the work turned into reality?

Include:

- Team model
- Operating rhythm
- Design approach
- Engineering collaboration
- Rollout strategy
- Quality mechanisms
- Governance

### 9. Outcomes

What changed?

Include metrics only if they can survive evidence review.

### 10. Lessons

What did this work teach?

Lessons should be useful beyond this specific story.

### 11. What I’d Do Differently

Show maturity.

This section prevents the story from sounding like self-promotion.

### 12. Frameworks Created

Did this work produce a reusable model, principle, operating mechanism, or framework?

Examples:

- Trust Stack
- Behavioral Growth Engine
- AI-native Design Maturity
- Product Confidence Pyramid
- Ambiguity to Outcome

### 13. Related Essays

Which essays can this story connect to?

Example:

- Why design systems fail
- Why trust is a product feature
- Why AI-native teams need new operating models

### 14. Related Operating Manual Pages

Which operating manual pages does this story connect to?

Example:

- Hiring
- Research
- Design Reviews
- Product Reviews
- AI Workflow
- Decision Making
- Stakeholder Management

### 15. Related Evidence

Which evidence files support this story?

Connect to:

- `EVIDENCE/CLAIMS_REGISTER.md`
- `EVIDENCE/METRICS_REGISTER.md`
- `EVIDENCE/CASE_EVIDENCE.md`
- `EVIDENCE/INTERVIEW_DEFENSE.md`
- `EVIDENCE/CONFIDENTIALITY_RULES.md`

### 16. Related Future Formats

How can this story compound?

Possible formats:

- Essay
- LinkedIn post
- X thread
- Talk
- Workshop
- YouTube video
- Course module
- Downloadable template
- Conference proposal
