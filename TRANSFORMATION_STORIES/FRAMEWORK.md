# FRAMEWORK.md

## Purpose

Defines the canonical structure of a transformation story. Transformation stories replace case studies. They communicate transformation, decisions, and tradeoffs — not process.

## Why Transformation Stories, Not Case Studies

A case study shows a process: problem, process, solution, impact.

A transformation story shows judgment: a system or business moved from one state to another, the decisions that moved it, and what was learned.

Executives are hired for judgment. The structure below forces judgment to the surface.

---

## The 12-Part Structure

Each transformation story must include:

1. **Context** — the situation, the company, the moment.
2. **Business Problem** — the measurable problem the business faced.
3. **Organizational Challenge** — the people, team, or system constraints.
4. **Constraints** — time, budget, politics, technical, regulatory.
5. **Research** — what was learned before acting.
6. **Decisions** — the key choices made, with reasoning.
7. **Tradeoffs** — what was given up, and why it was worth it.
8. **Execution** — how the work actually happened.
9. **Outcomes** — measurable results, linked to evidence.
10. **Lessons** — what generalizes beyond this story.
11. **What I'd Do Differently** — honest reflection.
12. **Frameworks Created** — reusable systems that came out of it.
