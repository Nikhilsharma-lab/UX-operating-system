# PAGES/HOME.md

## Purpose

This file defines the strategy for the homepage of the Nikhil Sharma Executive Knowledge System.

The homepage is not a portfolio landing page.

It is the front door to an executive knowledge system.

Its job is to make the right reader quickly understand:

1. Who Nikhil is
2. What he builds
3. Why he is different
4. What proof exists
5. Where to go next

The homepage should serve as a trailer for the larger system, not the whole movie.

# Homepage North Star

The homepage must pass two tests.

## 30-second recruiter test

A recruiter should understand:

- Nikhil is senior
- Nikhil operates at Head / Director-level product design leadership
- Nikhil works across design, product, AI, systems, and business outcomes
- Nikhil is worth contacting

## 5-minute VP Design test

A VP Design / CDO should understand:

- Nikhil can build product design organizations
- Nikhil thinks in systems, not screens
- Nikhil understands AI-native product development
- Nikhil has evidence of business and organizational impact
- Nikhil has a serious operating philosophy

---

# Homepage Thesis

The homepage should communicate this idea:

> Nikhil Sharma builds AI-native product organizations, systems, and businesses that turn ambiguity into measurable outcomes.

This is the homepage operating thesis.

The exact hero copy can vary, but the page must land this idea quickly.

---

# Homepage Role

The homepage is not supposed to explain everything.

It should:

- Establish identity
- Create trust
- Show proof
- Reveal depth
- Create paths
- Invite serious readers deeper

It should not:

- Show a grid of projects
- Open with screenshots
- Feel like a resume
- Feel like a generic UX portfolio
- Overexplain process
- Try to serve every audience equally
- Publish unsupported metrics
- Use fake testimonials
- Use decorative UI without purpose

---

# Primary Audiences

The homepage must primarily serve:

1. Recruiter
2. VP Design / Chief Design Officer
3. Founder / CEO
4. Product Leader

Secondary audiences:

5. Engineering Leader
6. AI Product Builder
7. Designer / Design Manager
8. Speaker / Conference Organizer

The homepage should be clear enough for recruiters and deep enough for senior evaluators.

# Homepage Structure v1

The v1 homepage should include the following sections in this order.

---

## 1. Navigation

Purpose:

Help readers quickly understand the system.

Navigation should feel minimal, editorial, and restrained.

Recommended primary nav:

- Capabilities
- Transformations
- Operating Manual
- Thinking
- Building
- Contact

Do not use:

- Portfolio
- Projects
- Case Studies
- About
- Blog

Possible label alternatives:

- Transformations instead of Transformation Stories if space is limited
- Manual instead of Operating Manual if visual rhythm requires shorter label

Navigation should not dominate the page.

---

## 2. Hero

Purpose:

Immediately establish identity, thesis, and difference.

The hero must answer:

- Who is Nikhil?
- What does he build?
- Why is this not a normal portfolio?

Hero should not open with:

- “Hi, I’m Nikhil”
- “Selected Work”
- “UX Portfolio”
- “I craft delightful experiences”
- “Product designer based in India”

Preferred hero direction:

```txt
I build AI-native product organizations that turn ambiguity into measurable outcomes.
```

Alternative hero directions:

```txt
Design creates systems that create products.
```

```txt
Building the operating system behind better products.
```

```txt
AI-native Product Executive building organizations, systems, and businesses.
```

Hero supporting copy should communicate:

- Head of Product Design / senior product design executive
- AI-native workflows
- Design organizations
- Product systems
- Business outcomes
- Builder/founder energy

Possible supporting copy:

```txt
I lead product design across regulated fintech, AI-native workflows, design systems, customer trust, and behavioral growth — building the teams, rituals, infrastructure, and products that help organizations ship with clarity.
```

Do not finalize public hero copy until evidence review is complete.

---

## 4. Belief / Brand Thesis

Purpose:

Introduce the intellectual spine of the site.

Core line:

```txt
Design creates systems that create products.
```

This section should explain:

- Products are visible outputs
- Organizations, hiring, research, AI workflows, rituals, and decision systems are the invisible systems behind them
- Nikhil’s work focuses on building those systems

This section should shift the reader from:

```txt
Show me your work.
```

to:

```txt
Show me how you think and operate.
```

## 5. Capabilities

Purpose:

Translate positioning into clear capability areas.

This section should answer:

What is Nikhil exceptional at?

Recommended capability cards:

1. **Organizational Design** — Building design teams, rituals, reviews, operating models, hiring systems, and leadership systems.
2. **AI-native Product Development** — Using AI to reshape product workflows, design-to-code collaboration, validation, and operating systems.
3. **Behavioral Growth** — Designing around real customer behavior to improve activation, adoption, and product outcomes.
4. **Customer Trust** — Building trust in regulated financial products through transparency, safety, fraud prevention, and product architecture.
5. **Product Infrastructure** — Design systems, component systems, product workflows, and implementation infrastructure that reduce friction.
6. **Builder / Founder Energy** — LANE, Svenklas, internal tools, AI experiments, and productized operating philosophy.

Each capability should link to:

- Relevant transformation stories
- Relevant operating manual pages
- Relevant frameworks

Role:

Core proof:

Audience served:

- Founder

- Process-step previews
- Weak labels like “View Project”

Use labels like:

- Read Transformation
- Study the System
- Explore the Decision

- 85% QA/UAT reduction
- 28× digital gold growth
- 7× onboarding growth
- 62% fraud reduction
- 39% fraud-related support handling
